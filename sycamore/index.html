Sycamore

A place of meeting and working
and building a life in common

We invite you to help shape the future of Sycamore, a big camelback double that has for seven years been an outpost of New Orleans counterpower.

Sycamore has done, been and survived many things; now we are looking to make the largest shift yet, larger than anything we've done before. We are expanding our vision, deepening our commitments and multiplying our capacities.

Who We Are

Sycamore is a "hub", a place to ground ourselves, to meet and build friendly energy against the domination of solitude. A hub tends to its own growth as well as the growth of others, each constructed in line with its makers' means, needs and desire for a meaningful version of happiness.

What We're Doing

By completing the renovation of the front of the house, we want to expand Sycamore to include a gathering hall, partisan library and more, expanding our resources as a space for encounter, experimentation and organization.

How We're Going To Do It

Over the course of eight weeks in September and October we can accomplish these ambitious goals... together. So it is we are writing to ask for your time, materials, skills, food, companionship and even money.

Here are some ways to contribute:

  • Money: All money will go towards the materials, tools and supplies needed to renovate and furnish the space.

  • Materials: In late August we'll be throwing a yard sale to raise money for construction; if you have any good stuff you'd like to contribute, let us know! Also we're still trying to purchase a large quantity of drywall and insulation -- about $350 and $200 worth, respectively. If you know of any good deals, do tell. Otherwise your donations will go directly to the purchase of these items.

  • Labor: We're seeking all sorts of help. For those with carpentry experience, let us know which jobs listed in the schedule suit your availability and skill-set. For those without experience in the work listed, let us know your availability and what you're interested in. Not only will each job require at least one semi-skilled or un-skilled helper, but there are a number of unlisted tasks that we need help with, such as daily meal preparation, general house maintenance, landscaping and building garden beds.
